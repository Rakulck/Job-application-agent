# Content Intelligence Skill — Slidez
## Principle-Based Writing System (Topic-Adaptive)

Load this before writing ANY carousel.
These are principles, not templates. When given a topic, Claude must think about what storytelling approach fits that specific topic best — not force it into a pre-built format.

---

## Step 0 — Read the Topic, Then Ask These 3 Questions

Before writing a single word, answer:

1. **What does the reader FEEL when they see this topic?**
   *(curious? annoyed? insecure? excited? skeptical?)*
   → Hook into that exact emotion. Not what YOU want to say — what THEY already feel.

2. **What is the single most surprising or useful thing about this topic?**
   → That's the center of gravity. Build the carousel toward it, then away from it.

3. **What would make someone save or send this?**
   → If nothing, change the angle until something would.

Only then, start writing.

---

## The One Rule That Never Changes

**Every slide does two jobs:**
1. Delivers one micro-reward (a small "oh wow", "yes exactly", or "I didn't know that")
2. Opens a new question the reader must swipe to close

This is the open loop system. Every slide ends with something unresolved.
The brain cannot leave an open question unanswered. That's the swipe.

---

## The Emotional Arc (This Structure Applies to Every Topic)

No matter what the topic is, every carousel follows this emotional journey:

```
Slide 1  →  INTERRUPT  — stop the scroll, open the biggest loop
Slide 2  →  VALIDATE   — prove the hook was real, earn their trust
Slide 3  →  TENSION    — deepen the problem or raise the stakes
Slide 4  →  SHIFT      — the first reveal, the "oh I see" moment
Slide 5  →  PROOF      — make it real, concrete, specific
Slide 6  →  DESIRE     — paint the "after", make them want the outcome
Slide 7  →  ACTION     — one ask, tied to what they just felt
```

The content on each slide changes based on topic. The arc never does.

---

## How to Write Each Position

### Slide 1 — INTERRUPT
The hook cover. Text only, no explanation.
- Make a bold claim, name a painful truth, or promise a surprising reveal
- Under 8 words
- Leave the biggest possible question unanswered

**What loop fits this topic?**
- Is the reader doing something wrong? → "YOU'VE BEEN {X} WRONG"
- Is there something they don't know? → "NOBODY TALKS {ABOUT THIS}"
- Did something just change? → "[THING] {JUST CHANGED}"
- Is there a transformation possible? → "THIS CHANGED {HOW I} SHOP"
- Is there a comparison or contrast? → "YOU VS SOMEONE {WHO USES} SLIDEZ"
- Is there a number/list? → "5 THINGS {YOU DON'T} KNOW YET"

Pick whichever loop type fits the topic. Don't force one.

---

### Slide 2 — VALIDATE
Instagram shows this slide first sometimes. It must stand alone.

**Job:** Make the reader feel understood. Prove the topic is real.
- Name the exact frustration or situation in THEIR words
- One specific detail that makes it feel lived-in, not generic
- End with a bridge that opens the next question

**Bridge formulas for slide 2:**
- *"And it's not your fault."*
- *"Here's what's actually happening."*
- *"Most people don't realise this."*
- *"But it doesn't have to be this way."*
- *"There's a reason for this."*

---

### Slide 3 — TENSION
Deepen the problem or raise what's at stake. Don't solve it yet.

**Job:** Make the reader feel the weight of the problem before you offer the fix.
- Add a layer they hadn't considered
- A stat, a consequence, or a contrast that makes the situation feel more urgent
- End with a bridge that hints at the turn coming

**Bridge formulas for slide 3:**
- *"But that's only part of it."*
- *"And the real cost is this."*
- *"Most people stop here. That's the mistake."*
- *"Until you know this, nothing changes."*
- *"And yet most people still do the opposite."*

---

### Slide 4 — SHIFT
The first real reveal. The "oh I see" moment.

**Job:** Flip the frame. Give them the insight that recontextualises everything before it.
- This is where Slidez enters — naturally, not forced
- It's the answer to the tension, not a feature dump
- End with a bridge that opens the next layer

**Bridge formulas for slide 4:**
- *"But the feature nobody talks about is this."*
- *"And here's where it gets interesting."*
- *"This is exactly why we built Slidez."*
- *"Most apps stop here. We didn't."*
- *"And that's just the start."*

---

### Slide 5 — PROOF
Make it concrete. Specific. Real.

**Job:** Take the insight from slide 4 and ground it in a real scenario, number, or example.
- A specific use case: "You're getting ready for a date. You open Slidez..."
- A specific number: "In 3 seconds, you're looking at yourself in the outfit."
- A specific comparison: "Other apps show a mannequin. This shows you."
- End with a bridge toward the "after" picture

**Bridge formulas for slide 5:**
- *"Here's what that looks like in real life."*
- *"This is what the difference actually feels like."*
- *"And the result?"*
- *"Sound too simple? It is. That's the point."*

---

### Slide 6 — DESIRE
The "after" picture. Make them feel the outcome before they have it.

**Job:** Give them a vivid, personal, specific vision of their life with this solved.
- Write it in second person: "You open Slidez. You describe the vibe..."
- Make it sensory and specific, not abstract
- No feature names. Just the feeling.
- This slide has NO bridge — it ends in satisfaction, not a question

Example:
> "You open Slidez. Tell it: date night, smart casual, something that actually fits.
> Ten seconds later you're looking at yourself in a real outfit from a real brand.
> Not a mannequin. You.
> That's what it feels like to stop guessing."

---

### Slide 7 — ACTION
One action only. Tied to the emotion of slide 6.

**Never:** just "Try Slidez free"
**Always:** connect the action to what they just felt

Formulas:
- Save: *"Save this — you'll want it before your next event."*
- Share: *"Send this to someone who still guesses what to wear."*
- Follow: *"Follow @slidez — we post this every week."*
- Try: *"You just saw how it works. Try it free — link in bio 👆"*
- Comment: *"Comment your next occasion. We'll style it."*
- DM: *"DM us 'STYLE' and we'll send you your first look."*

## The Bridge Library (Use These Between Slides)

These lines close the current thought and open the next one simultaneously.
Every body slide must end with one — or a variation.

**Opening curiosity:**
- *"But here's what most people miss."*
- *"What nobody tells you is..."*
- *"And there's a reason this works."*
- *"Until now."*

**Raising stakes:**
- *"And it's costing you."*
- *"This is why returns exist."*
- *"Most people never figure this out."*
- *"The old way doesn't work anymore."*

**Hinting at the reveal:**
- *"Slide [N] is where it changes."*
- *"And here's where Slidez comes in."*
- *"The fix is simpler than you think."*
- *"Here's what changes when you do."*

**After a reveal:**
- *"But that's only half of it."*
- *"And it gets better."*
- *"Here's the part nobody expects."*
- *"Now here's the real proof."*

**Pivoting to desire:**
- *"Here's what that actually feels like."*
- *"Imagine this next time you get dressed."*
- *"This is the difference."*

## Voice Rules — Always Apply These

Slidez is a smart, stylish friend who knows AI. Not a brand, not a news outlet.

**The test:** Read each slide as if you're texting someone. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.

| ❌ Brand/news voice | ✅ Slidez voice |
|---|---|
| "AI styling technology enables personalised recommendations" | "Tell AI what you're going for. It handles the rest." |
| "Users can import outfits from external platforms" | "See something on TikTok? Share it straight to Slidez." |
| "Slidez provides a virtual try-on experience" | "You can see the outfit on you before you buy it." |
| "The fashion industry is experiencing significant disruption" | "The way people shop clothes just changed." |

**Non-negotiable:**
- "You" and "your" on every slide
- Short sentences. Fragments are fine.
- Contractions always: "it's" not "it is"
- Numbers build credibility: "3 seconds", "one photo", "10,000 brands"
- Rhetorical questions pull people in: *"Sound familiar?"* *"Ever done this?"*
- Never start a slide with "In this slide..." or "Today I want to..."

## Topic Intake — How Claude Should Think Through Any Topic

When given a topic like *"the share-to-import feature"* or *"why AI styling is the future"* or *"how Slidez works"* — Claude should NOT look for which of the 5 formats it fits into. Instead:

**1. Find the emotional angle first**
What does someone FEEL about this topic before they know the solution?
- Frustrated? Skeptical? Curious? Excited? Insecure?

**2. Find the tension**
What is the gap between where they are and where they want to be?
That gap is your carousel.

**3. Find the "oh wow" moment**
What is the single most surprising or counterintuitive thing about this topic?
That moment belongs on slide 4 or 5. Build toward it.

**4. Choose the best opening loop for THIS topic**
Don't assign a format. Ask: what is the most compelling way to open a loop on this specific topic?

**5. Write the arc**
Interrupt → Validate → Tension → Shift → Proof → Desire → Action

## Topic Examples — How the Arc Changes Per Topic

**Topic: "share-to-import feature"**
- Emotional angle: frustration with copy-pasting links
- Tension: the friction of getting outfit inspo into an app
- "Oh wow": you can literally share from any app with no link
- Opening loop: *"OTHER APPS MAKE {YOU WORK} FOR IT"*

**Topic: "virtual try-on vs guessing"**
- Emotional angle: insecurity about buying clothes online
- Tension: the body type problem — clothes look different on you vs the model
- "Oh wow": see it on your actual photo, not a mannequin
- Opening loop: *"YOU'VE BEEN {BUYING} BLIND"*

**Topic: "AI styling is the future"**
- Emotional angle: excitement mixed with skepticism
- Tension: most AI fashion feels generic and impersonal
- "Oh wow": Slidez uses real brand items not generic outfits
- Opening loop: *"AI {STYLING} CHANGED. HERE'S WHAT'S DIFFERENT"*

**Topic: "how people use Slidez before travel"**
- Emotional angle: the stress of packing and planning outfits
- Tension: buying new clothes before a trip and regretting it
- "Oh wow": style an entire trip in minutes, no purchases needed yet
- Opening loop: *"HOW I {PACKED} FOR ITALY IN 10 MINUTES"*

**Topic: "Slidez vs other styling apps"**
- Emotional angle: disappointment with apps that overpromise
- Tension: other apps show generic looks, not your actual body
- "Oh wow": real brands, real photo of you, no fakes
- Opening loop: *"EVERY {OTHER} STYLING APP LIED TO YOU"*

Notice — none of these are the same format. Each topic gets its own angle based on what fits.

## The "So What?" Test

After writing every slide, say "so what?" out loud as the reader.

- *"AI styling is a growing trend in fashion."* → So what?
- *"You just saved 40 minutes of outfit panic."* → I want that.

If "so what?" doesn't have an obvious answer inside the slide — rewrite it.

---

## Final Check Before Generating

1. Does slide 1 leave a question that makes NOT swiping feel like a loss?
2. Does slide 2 make the reader feel understood?
3. Does every body slide end with an unresolved bridge?
4. Is there ONE clear "oh wow" moment in the carousel?
5. Does slide 6 make the reader feel the outcome before they have it?
6. Is the CTA tied to the emotion of slide 6 — not just a generic prompt?
7. Does it sound like a person texting a friend — not a brand posting content?

If all 7 are yes — generate. If not — fix the nos first.
